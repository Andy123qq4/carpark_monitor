Quick update on the project — I met with Prof. Kenneth Leung (SUST Director) on Monday

---

His biggest point: this is NOT just an AI project. It's a business workflow problem. The judges are Sino management, they care about feasibility and cost savings, not fancy tech

---

Here's what Kenneth suggested as the approach:

Step 1 — Force all vehicles to register before using the loading bay. Plate number + payment method. Can use Sino's existing property app, just add a new module

Step 2 — Month 1, put a guard at the gate to enforce registration. "You want to do business here? Register first." Property owner has the right to set rules

Step 3 — Once most vehicles are registered, AI takes over. Camera reads plate → check database → auto-log time → auto-charge if over 20 min. If unregistered vehicle shows up → instant alert to staff to go register them on-site

Step 4 — Over time, fewer unregistered vehicles = less human work needed. Show this reduction to judges — that's the cost savings pitch

Step 5 — Bonus: data analytics. Parking duration, peak hours, repeat offenders, revenue reports. Kenneth said judges will love this

---

His key quote: "You can't solve 100%, but you solve it progressively"
